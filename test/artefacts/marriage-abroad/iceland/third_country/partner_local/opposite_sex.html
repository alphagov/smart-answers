Getting married abroad

Marriage in Iceland

Contact the relevant local authorities in Iceland to find out about local marriage laws, including what documents you’ll need.

You should get legal advice and check the travel advice for Iceland before making any plans.

What you need to do

You may be asked to provide a certificate of no impediment (CNI) or a similar document to prove you’re allowed to marry.

There are 2 ways you can get a CNI:

  • go to the UK and post notice with a UK registrar
  • go to Iceland and post notice at the embassy or consulate there

If you choose to post notice in Iceland, you’ll normally have to wait at least 10 days before you can get married.

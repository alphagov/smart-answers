You might be able to increase the amount of [basic State Pension](/state-pension) to £67.8 per week if:

- your spouse or civil partner has enough National Insurance contributions
- your spouse or civil partner was born on or after 6 April 1950 (this rule doesn’t apply to you if you’re a woman married to a man)

One of the following must also apply:

- you don’t qualify for any basic State Pension
- your basic State pension is less than £67.8 per week  

You’ll get the increase when either you or your spouse or civil partner reach State Pension age, whichever is later.

##If you become widowed or your civil partner dies

You may be able to increase your basic State Pension up to £113.1 a week if:

- your own basic State Pension is less than £113.1 a week
- your late spouse or civil partner had enough National Insurance contributions

You may also inherit a portion of their [Additional State-Pension](/additional-state-pension).

^You won’t be able to increase your basic State Pension or inherit any Additional State Pension if you remarry or form a new civil partnership before you reach State Pension age.^

You might also be eligible for [Widowed Parent’s Allowance](/widowed-parents-allowance), a [Bereavement payment](/bereavement-payment) or [Bereavement Allowance](/bereavement-allowance) if you haven’t reached State Pension age.

##If you divorce your spouse or civil partner

You may be able to increase your basic State Pension up to £113.1 a week if:

- your own [basic State Pension](/state-pension) is less than £113.1
- your divorced spouse or civil partner had enough National Insurance contributions (up to when your marriage or civil partnership ended)

^You won’t get the increase if you remarry or form a new civil partnership before you reach State Pension age.^

##Increase your retirement income

You might be able to increase your State Pension amount if:

- you’re eligible for [National Insurance credits](/national-insurance-credits)
- you make [voluntary National Insurance contributions](/voluntary-national-insurance-contributions)
- you choose to defer your State Pension

You can also contribute to [workplace pension](/workplace-pensions) and [personal or private pensions](/personal-pensions-your-rights).

^Contact the [Pension Service](/contact-pension-service) for help and advice.^
